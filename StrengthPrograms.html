At Home Strength Workout Programs

Why Workout at Home?

Working out at home is a huge time convenience and saves you a lot of money. Working out at gyms can also be a judgemental environment. Using our programs in the comfort of your own home allows you to workout judgement free.










The First Month, Building Muscle

This month is all about getting building muslce using no equipment at all. All you need is a space in your house and that is IT. There will be many bodyweight exercises that you will be familiar with along with others that you may not be so familiar with. However, don't worry, our programs provide you with videos that explain to you how to do each exercise. There will also be ab exercises after each workout which will test you mentally, but getting through it will get you those 6-pack abs that you have been craving. If you stick with this plan, you will not only get stronger, but you may even be stronger than your friends who go to a actual gym. It sounds crazy, but if you follow this plan, you will know what I am talking about.


The Second Month, Getting Explosive

This plan is just like the first month plan in the sense that you will only need a small space and no equipment at all. However, this plan is working to get you more explosive, improve your balance, speed, and jump height. This plan will test you and is a bit more challenging than month one. This plan also includes quick feet exercises before the workout and abs after the workout. I reccommend this program for athletes because being explosive is key toward improving your success on the athletic field.

You will need to know these 5 things before getting started.

  1. You will need a space in your house, room, garage, or any type of space that is comfortable for you to workout in.
  2. Throughout this program you will see links at the top of each week. These links show explanation videos that teaches you how to do each exercise. You can click on these links that will have videos that will explain to you how to do each workout.
  3. STRETCH. You must always stretch before you begin your workout. This will help prevent injury and help you get the maximum output of your workout.
  4. The number of reps and sets is provided, but not set and stone. If you find the number of reps and sets is either too easy or too difficult, manage it so it can be beneficial to you. They are only provided to help give you an idea as to where to start, think of it as a recommendation.
  5. Some of these workouts may be tough, it is important to push through. However, if there are exercises that you cannot do, change them up so you can do a similar form of the exercise.
